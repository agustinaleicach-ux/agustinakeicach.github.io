📝 Grammar Cheat Sheet

✅ Past Simple – Regular Verbs

  • walk → walked  |  play → played  |  stop → stopped
  • I walked to school yesterday.
  • Negative: She didn't play tennis last week.
  • Question: Did you watch TV? — Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.

✅ Past Simple – Irregular Verbs

  • go → went  |  see → saw  |  eat → ate
  • have → had  |  make → made  |  run → ran

✅ Comparative Adjectives

  • Short: fast → faster, big → bigger, happy → happier
  • Long: beautiful → more beautiful
  • Irregular: good → better, bad → worse

📰 Student Tips

Past Tense Superstar: Kids 3 Tips!

In Kids 3 you'll travel back in time with the Past Simple! It's one of the most important grammar points in English. Here's how to master it. 🕰️

📓 Past Simple diary

Every evening, write 3 sentences about what you did today. It's the best way to practise Past Simple naturally.

💡 Irregular verb trick: Make flashcards! Write the base form on one side and the past form on the other. Try to learn 3 new irregular verbs each week. There are only about 50 common ones!

🔍 Irregular verb hunt

As you read The Selfish Giant, find every Past Simple verb and sort them into two columns: Regular (-ed) and Irregular. Then test yourself!

📖 Your Reader: The Selfish Giant

This is a beautiful story with lots of comparatives. Look for words like bigger, colder, sadder, more beautiful. Can you find at least 10 adjectives in comparative form?

⚔️ Comparative battles

  • Is a cat faster than a dog?
  • Is summer hotter than winter?
  • Is English more interesting than maths?

Write your opinions and share them with a classmate! There are no wrong answers here — just use the right grammar.
