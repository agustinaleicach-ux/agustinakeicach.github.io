📝 Grammar Cheat Sheet

✅ Present Perfect

  • I have visited London. (ever in life)
  • She has never eaten sushi.
  • Have you ever seen a whale? — Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.
  • Key words: ever, never, already, yet, just

✅ Past Simple vs Present Perfect

  • Past Simple: I visited London last year. (specific time)
  • Present Perfect: I have visited London. (no specific time)

✅ Modal Verbs: should / shouldn't

  • You should exercise every day. (it's a good idea)
  • You shouldn't eat junk food. (it's a bad idea)

✅ If clauses (Zero Conditional)

  • If you heat water, it boils.
  • If it rains, I stay inside.

📰 Student Tips

Getting Ready for Teens: Kids 5 Tips!

Welcome to Kids 5 — the final Kids level! This year you grow into the most advanced young learners at the institute. You're bridging into Teen territory! 🏆

🌍 Have you ever…?

The Present Perfect is perfect for talking about life experiences. Play "Have you ever…?" with a friend — take turns asking questions. Make it fun with wild scenarios!

💡 Example game: "Have you ever eaten a whole pizza? / Have you ever met a famous person? / Have you ever slept in a tent?" If you answer YES, you must say when: "Yes, I have! I did it last summer."

🚗 Your Reader: A Little Trouble in California

This road-trip adventure is set in the USA! As you read, make a list of every place mentioned. Then look them up on a map. Can you trace the characters' journey?

🩺 Should / Shouldn't advice columns

Write an "advice column" in English: invent 5 problems and give advice for each one using should and shouldn't. Example:

  • "I have a headache." → You should rest. You shouldn't look at screens."
  • "I'm nervous about my exam." → You should study a little every day. You shouldn't panic!"
