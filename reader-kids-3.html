Primary Readers Level 2 · MM Publications · Based on Oscar Wilde

📖 About the Book

Original Author
Oscar Wilde
Series
MM Primary Readers
Genre
🌸 Classic / Fantasy
Theme
❤️ Kindness & Sharing
A giant has a beautiful garden, but he is selfish — he doesn't let any children play in it. He builds a tall wall and the garden becomes cold and sad forever. Then one day, a small boy changes everything. Will the giant learn to be kind? 🌱

👥 Characters

🧟
The Giant
Big and selfish at first. He changes by the end of the story.
👦
The Little Boy
A small, mysterious child who changes the giant's heart.
🧒
The Children
They love playing in the garden but the giant sends them away.
❄️
Winter
Always stays in the garden while the giant is selfish.

🗺️ Story Map

🏡 Beginning: The giant returns home and finds children playing in his garden. He gets very angry and builds a big wall. Spring never comes to his garden.
🌱 Middle: One day the giant hears birds singing and sees flowers. Through a hole in the wall, the children came back — and with them, spring! One small boy can't climb a tree.
💚 End: The giant helps the little boy and knocks down the wall. He becomes kind and shares his garden. Years later, the mysterious boy returns and the giant understands everything. 🌸

✍️ Creative Activities

📰 Activity 1 – The Giant's Diary (Past Simple)

Write a diary entry for the giant on the day he helped the little boy. Use Past Simple verbs (walked, saw, felt, helped, knocked…). Write 5–6 sentences.

🌡️ Activity 2 – Season Comparisons

In the story, the giant's garden in winter is sadder and colder than in spring. Compare the two seasons using comparative adjectives (bigger, more beautiful, warmer, colder…). Write 6 sentences.

💌 Activity 3 – A Letter of Apology

Write a letter from the giant to the children, apologising for being selfish. Use Past Simple to say what he did wrong and going to for his promises. Write 6–7 sentences.
