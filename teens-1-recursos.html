📝 Grammar Summary

✅ Present Simple & Continuous

TenseUseExample
Present SimpleHabits, facts, routinesShe plays tennis on Saturdays.
Present ContinuousRight now / temporaryShe is playing tennis now.

✅ Past Simple

  • Regular: walk → walked, study → studied
  • Irregular: go → went, see → saw, have → had
  • Negative: I didn't go. Question: Did you go?

✅ There is / There are · Some / Any

  • There is some milk. (+ sentences)
  • Is there any milk? / There isn't any milk. (? / − sentences)

✅ Comparative & Superlative

  • fast → faster → the fastest
  • beautiful → more beautiful → the most beautiful
  • good → better → the best  |  bad → worse → the worst

✅ Modal Verbs: can / must / should

  • I can swim. / She can't drive.
  • You must wear a uniform. / You mustn't run.
  • You should study more. / You shouldn't eat junk food.

📰 Student Tips

Welcome to Teens 1! Your English Journey Starts Now 🚀

Moving from Kids to Teens is a big step — and you're ready for it! In Teens 1 you'll use Prepare Level 1, a book full of real topics that teenagers care about: school, friends, free time, travel and more.

📅 Daily English habits

The best Teens 1 students practise a little every day. Here's a simple daily plan:

  • ☀️ Morning: say 5 sentences in English about your day ahead
  • 📱 During the day: try to think in English for 5 minutes
  • 🌙 Evening: write 3 sentences about what you did (Past Simple!)
💡 Grammar shortcut: The hardest thing about Past Simple is irregular verbs. Make a list of 5 new irregular verbs each week and test yourself on Friday. By the end of the year you'll know 200+ irregular verbs!

📖 Your Reader: Newspaper Chase

Your reader this year is a fast-paced crime story. As you read, notice how the author uses Past Simple to tell the story — that's exactly the grammar you're practising in class!

🎯 Exam skills to build now

  • Reading: Read the questions before the text — know what you're looking for
  • Listening: Focus on key words, not every single word
  • Writing: Always plan before you write — 2 minutes of planning saves 10 minutes of crossing out
  • Speaking: If you don't know a word, describe it! "It's a thing you use for..." is a great strategy
