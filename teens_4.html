📝 Grammar Summary

✅ Third Conditional

  • If I had studied harder, I would have passed.
  • If she hadn't missed the bus, she would have arrived on time.
  • Use: imaginary past situations and their consequences

✅ Mixed Conditionals

  • If I had studied medicine, I would be a doctor now.
  • (past condition → present result)

✅ Wish / If only

  • I wish I were taller. (present regret)
  • I wish I had studied more. (past regret)
  • If only I could fly! (impossible wish)

✅ Advanced Passive Forms

  • It is believed that… / It is said that…
  • The research is being conducted. (Present Continuous Passive)
  • The results have been published. (Present Perfect Passive)

📰 Student Tips

Teens 4 — Let's Level Up! 🔬

In Teens 4 you're approaching B2 level — that's the standard for the Cambridge First Certificate! This year's toughest grammar is the Third Conditional.

🧩 Third Conditional formula

If + had + past participle, would have + past participle

💡 Shortcut: Think of Third Conditional as "looking back with regret." Something happened (or didn't happen) in the past, and you're imagining the alternative.
