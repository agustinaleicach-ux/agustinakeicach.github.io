📝 Grammar Cheat Sheet

✅ Present Continuous

  • I am playing football right now.
  • She is reading a book at the moment.
  • We are not watching TV.
  • Question: Are they swimming? — Yes, they are.

✅ Present Simple vs Present Continuous

  • Simple: I play tennis every Saturday. (habit)
  • Continuous: I am playing tennis now. (right now)

✅ Superlative Adjectives

  • Short: fast → the fastest, big → the biggest
  • Long: expensive → the most expensive
  • Irregular: good → the best, bad → the worst

✅ Going to (future plans)

  • I am going to visit my grandma tomorrow.
  • She is not going to play football.

📰 Student Tips

Future Plans & Present Action: Kids 4 Tips!

In Kids 4 you're growing into a confident English speaker. This year's big grammar topics — Present Continuous and Going To — are everywhere in real life!

📸 Present Continuous live commentary

Try this: narrate what you're doing as if you're a sports commentator! "I am sitting on my chair. I am opening my book. I am writing in English." It feels silly but it works!

💡 Going To tip: Plan your weekend in English on Friday evening. Write 5 sentences: "On Saturday I am going to…" Share it with your teacher on Monday!

🐬 Your Reader: Maisie and the Dolphin

Look for superlatives as you read: the biggest, the most beautiful, the fastest. Ocean animals are perfect for superlatives — can you rank 5 ocean animals using adjectives?

🏆 Superlative challenge

  • What is the tallest animal in the world? 🦒
  • What is the most dangerous animal? 🦈
  • What is the fastest bird? 🦅
  • What is the smallest mammal? 🐭

Research the answers in English and bring them to class!

📆 Habits vs now

Practice the difference every day: pick one thing you do as a habit (simple) and one thing you're doing right now (continuous). Write both sentences!
