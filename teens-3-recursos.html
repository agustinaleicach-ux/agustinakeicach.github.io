📝 Grammar Summary

✅ Second Conditional

  • If I were rich, I would travel the world.
  • If she had more time, she would study harder.
  • Use: imaginary / unlikely present or future situations

✅ Reported Speech

DirectReported
"I am tired."She said she was tired.
"I will come."He said he would come.
"I have finished."She said she had finished.

✅ Relative Clauses

  • who → for people: The man who called is my uncle.
  • which / that → for things: The book that I read was amazing.
  • where → for places: The city where I grew up is beautiful.

✅ Modal Verbs for Deduction

  • She must be tired — she worked all night. (certain)
  • He might be home. (possible)
  • They can't be serious! (impossible)

📰 Student Tips

Teens 3 — Let's Level Up! 🗺️

In Teens 3 you bridge to upper-intermediate grammar. The hardest structure this year is Reported Speech — the key is remembering that verbs "go back" one tense in time.

🎭 Reported Speech hack

  • am/is/are → was/were
  • will → would
  • have/has → had
  • can → could
💡 Second Conditional: Always use "were" (not "was") for all subjects in formal writing: "If I were you..." even though many native speakers say "was" in casual speech.
