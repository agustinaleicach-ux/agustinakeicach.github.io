📝 Grammar Cheat Sheet

✅ Plural Nouns

  • book → books  |  box → boxes  |  city → cities

✅ There is / There are

  • There is a cat on the mat.
  • There are three books on the desk.

✅ This / That / These / Those

  • This is my bag. (near, singular)
  • That is your book. (far, singular)
  • These are my pencils. (near, plural)
  • Those are her pens. (far, plural)

✅ Possessive Adjectives

my · your · his · her · our · their

📰 Student Tips

Brighter Ideas = Bigger English!

You're in Kids 1 — great news! This year you'll explore amazing topics like animals, food, family, and school. Here's how to shine ✨

🐾 Animals topic

Your book is full of animals. Make your own "animal dictionary": draw each animal and write its name in English underneath. Add one fact about it!

💡 Fun challenge: Can you learn the names of 10 animals in English this week? Try: cat, dog, bird, fish, rabbit, horse, elephant, lion, tiger, monkey!

📖 Your Reader: The Princess and the Frog

This classic story is fantastic for practising adjectives (big, small, green, golden…) and verbs (jump, kiss, run). As you read, highlight all the adjectives you find!

🏠 Practise at home

  • Label objects in your room with sticky notes in English.
  • Point to things and say: "This is my… / That is my…"
  • Count things around the house out loud.
  • Ask your parents: "What is this in English?"

🌈 Grammar trick: There is / There are

Look around any room and make sentences: "There is a lamp. There are three chairs." It's the best way to practise this grammar!
