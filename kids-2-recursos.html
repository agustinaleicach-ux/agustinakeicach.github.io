📝 Grammar Cheat Sheet

✅ Present Simple

  • I/You/We/They play football.
  • He/She/It plays football. (add -s/-es)
  • Negative: I don't like fish. / She doesn't like fish.
  • Question: Do you like pizza? / Does he play tennis?

✅ Adverbs of Frequency

always (100%) · usually · often · sometimes · rarely · never (0%)

  • I always brush my teeth. / She never eats meat.

✅ Can / Can't

  • I can swim. / She can't ride a bike.
  • Can you sing? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't.

📰 Student Tips

Level Up with Brighter Ideas 2!

Welcome to Kids 2! You already know a lot of English — now it's time to say more sentences and talk about your daily life. You've got this! 💜

📅 Daily routines

One of the big topics this year is daily routines. Try keeping a "daily routine journal" in English: write 3 sentences every day about what you do. Use adverbs of frequency!

💡 Example journal entry: "I always wake up at 7 o'clock. I sometimes eat cereal for breakfast. I never watch TV in the morning."

🎭 Can you…? game

Play this with a friend or family member: take turns asking "Can you…?" questions. The other person answers with "Yes, I can!" or "No, I can't." Try 10 questions!

📖 Your Reader: Jasper's Pot of Gold

This story is full of adventure and surprises. Look for all the verbs as you read — write them down and sort them into "things I can do" and "things I can't do yet."

🗓️ Frequency challenge

  • Use always for 3 habits you never skip.
  • Use sometimes for 3 things you do a few times a week.
  • Use never for 3 things you don't do at all.

Write them in your notebook and share with your teacher!
